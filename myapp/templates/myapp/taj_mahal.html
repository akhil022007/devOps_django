The Magnificent Taj Mahal

A timeless symbol of love and architectural grandeur.

The Taj Mahal is a renowned white marble mausoleum located in Agra, India, built by Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his wife Mumtaz Mahal. It's a UNESCO World Heritage site and is considered one of the world's most beautiful and iconic buildings, showcasing Mughal architecture.

Key Aspects of the Taj Mahal:

  • Symbol of Love: The Taj Mahal is widely recognized as a symbol of eternal love, built after Mumtaz Mahal's death.
  • Mughal Architecture: It represents the finest example of Mughal architecture, blending Persian, Turkish, and Indian styles.
  • Materials and Construction: The mausoleum is made of white marble, with intricate carvings and precious stones. Construction involved thousands of artisans and took nearly 20 years.
  • Complex and Gardens: The Taj Mahal complex includes the main mausoleum, a mosque, a guesthouse, and formal gardens.
  • UNESCO World Heritage Site: The Taj Mahal was designated a UNESCO World Heritage site in 1983.
  • Tourism: It is a major tourist attraction, drawing visitors from around the world.
  • Location: It is situated on the banks of the Yamuna River in Agra.
